About Kosgoda Beach

In the southwest of Sri Lanka, there is a stunning coastline region called Kosgoda Beach. It is well known for its gorgeous golden sand beach, crystal-clear waters, and tranquil environs. In addition to being a well-liked tourist site, Kosgoda Beach is important for the preservation of sea turtles.





Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation & Research Center


The sea turtle hatcheries in Kosgoda mainly use the same methods as other ones in Sri Lanka. They remove sea turtle eggs from shoreline nests that are at risk and place them in secure parts of the facility. By lowering the likelihood of predation and human meddling, this increases the likelihood that the eggs and hatchlings will survive. The young are housed in tanks after the eggs hatch until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. Injured turtles are also treated and rehabilitated at the hatcheries before being released back into the wild.


Sea turtle eggs are relocated to sheltered spaces inside the hatchery from nesting locations along the shore. By doing this, the eggs are protected from both natural predators and human disruptions. The young are housed in tanks after the eggs hatch until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. Injured turtles are also looked after at the hatchery and given medical attention before being returned to their natural habitat.



More Over...

Beyond conservation initiatives, Kosgoda Beach provides beach enthusiasts with a tranquil and attractive surroundings. The wide expanse of the Indian Ocean's picturesque magnificence can be enjoyed while relaxing, sunbathing, and playing in the soft sand. Swimming is permitted due to the calm and mild waves, but use caution as the currents can change.
